It Ain't All Robots

The first thing most people imagine when they hear 'automation' is robots, or more specifically robotic arms; typically installed on a factory floor, undertaking mass scale fabrication.

In reality, robots (including robotic arms) only account for a very small proportion of automated systems.

Automation is simply a means by which a task is performed with little to no human input.

Ultimately, any task can be automated. However, some tasks are more suited than others and the benefits of automating a task should be weighed against the solution required.

Automation systems vary significantly in scale and complexity;

  • from entirely software (e.g. image processing) to entirely hardware (e.g. hardwired circuits)
  • from small embedded systems to large Plant Control Systems
  • from a single task step to complete continuous autonomous operation.

So whether its robotic arms or something else; we're here to help.
