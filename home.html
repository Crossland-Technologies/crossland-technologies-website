It Ain't All Robots

The first thing most people imagine when they hear 'automation' is robots, or more specifically robotic arms; typically installed on a factory floor, undertaking mass scale fabrication.

In reality, robots (including robotic arms) only account for a very small proportion of automated systems.

Automation is simply a means by which a task is performed with little to no human input.

Ultimately, any task can be automated. However, some tasks are more suited than others and the benefits of automating a task should be weighed against the solution required.

Automation systems vary significantly in scale and complexity;

  • from entirely software (e.g. image processing) to entirely hardware (e.g. hardwired circuits)
  • from small embedded systems to large Plant Control Systems
  • from a single task step to complete continuous autonomous operation.

So whether its robotic arms or something else; we're here to help.

Brain To Paper

The first step is to generate a conceptual design.

A conceptual design is a high level description of your idea and how it works; we want to get what's in your brain on to paper.

The scope and size of the conceptual design depends upon;

  • the size of your organisation,
  • the complexity of your idea.
  • the type of industry.

Conceptual designs are a crucial first step as they allow all stakeholders to operate from a common point.

For entrepreneurs and inventors conceptual deigns can provide credibility and legitimacy with investors.

For project managers conceptual designs provide re-assurance to stakeholders.

Our experience shows that getting the conceptual design right at the start is crucial for success.

Our conceptual design service consists:

  1. A kick-off meeting (face-to-face or video call) where we will discuss your idea.
  2. We will send you your tailored conceptual design questionnaire.
  3. Once the questionnaire is returned, we will produce your conceptual design.
  4. You then get chance to review your new conceptual design.
  5. We will update your design with any comments you may have.
  6. With your conceptual design you're ready to approach the world to make your idea a reality.

3D Artwork

The famous addage 'a picture paints a thousand words' is true and especially so for 3D artwork.

We produce 3D imagery, animations and videos to help you clearly and concisely convey your idea.

We highly recommend you to use 3D artwork supports your conceptual design.

How it works (products / ideas / inventions):

  1. The first step is to model your product or idea in 3D, this means getting the physical dimensions correct.
  2. Once you approve the model dimensions we can work on appearance; get the colours and textures correct.
    • How far you go with the aesthetics of the model is personal preference, our guidance is to remember the purpose of the 3D artwork.
    • If the intention is to give a sense of scale or function only then aesthetics are not as important.
    • If the intention is for the model to be used in a product video then aesthetics are extremely important.
  3. Once the physical dimensions are correct, should you wish, you can also have your model 3D printed (full size or small scale).
  4. With the model dimensionally and aesthetically correct we can then use the model to produce the artwork you require:
    • Static images and/or diagrams (typically used to support conceptual design.)
    • Product placement images, where we place your 3D model into real photographs. (Typically used for investors & stakeholders, but also give a useful sense of scale.)
    • Animations, demonstrating how your idea works or is assembled. (Especially useful if the workings/assembly are not immediately apparent.)
    • Product Video, advertising your idea. (Typically used for websites and attracting customers).
  5. As well as individual discrete items we can also create scenes and background objects:
    • Static background images again can be used to give a good sense of scale.
    • Live construction plans - when updated regularly the 3D scene can be used to determine implementation routes and installation orders (e.g. process pipework and ducting).
    • Scene animations can convey construction order and/or fly by views.
    • Walkthrough videos can be created to show exactly how the final idea or concept will be implemented.

3D artwork not only gives a sense of legitimacy that does not come from 2D drawings but they can also be truly efficient means of conveying your idea to others.

Whether you want to wow your investors, teach new staff, support a design review or advertise; our 3D artwork services are here to help.

2D Artwork

It doesn't matter how well written a piece of text is, sometimes, it's just easier to draw it. A good drawing will capture the attention of your audience and compel them to delve deeper into the detail.

Our 2D artwork services are typically used to support conceptual design where clear, concise and accurate representations of your idea are imperative.

Examples of our 2D artwork include:

  • Process Flow Diagrams
    • Showing how your process or idea will work.
    • Showing how your process or idea will be assembled or constructed.
    • Showing the user journey for your process or idea.
  • User Interface Mock Ups
  • Schematic Representations
    • Functional Block Diagrams
    • System Integration Plans
    • Layouts

Our 2D artwork is intended to be used to support understanding and discussion and is not intended to be used as detailed design documentation. We offer a number of design services that produce this information.

Technical Design

Our technical design service provdes you with the expertise you need to get your system automated, from consultation to documentation. As with the conceptual design, the size of your design package will depend greatly upon the size, scale and complexity of your idea as well as the industry within which you operate.

Unlike the conceptual design which is concerned with explaining the idea, our technical design services are concerned with implementation - the hows and the whats:

  • How will the item be constructed.
  • How will the system and sub systems perform their function.
  • How will the item be operated.
  • How will the system respond to failure.
  • What materials are required.
  • What safety systems are required.
  • What is acceptable (pass) what is not acceptable (fail).

We have years of experience of delivering design packages for ideas and projects ranging from thousands of pounds to millions of pounds. We have experience working within highly regulated sectors and undertaken all aspects within the design life cycle.

A selection of typical design package deliverables are shown below, the list is by no means exhaustive:

Documents Drawings* Calculations
  • Operating Manuals
  • Process Descriptions
  • Hazard & Operability Studies
  • Feasibility Studies
  • Optioneering Studies
  • Requirement Specifications
  • Software Specifications
  • Testing Specifications
  • Piping & Instrumentation Drawings (P&IDs)
  • Process Flow Diagrams
  • Network Overviews
  • Electrical Schematics
  • PCB Designs
  • Mechanical Drawings
  • Single Line Diagrams
  • Boolean Logic
  • Process Modelling / Simulation
  • Failure Rate Determination
  • Availability Calculations
  • Power Requirements

*All of our 2D drawings are produced in EPLAN as standard.

If you are new to automation, we can advise you on the items you are likely to need.

If your organisation has different design deliverables and processes we are happy to accommodate these too.

Ultimately your design package needs enough information and detail to allow the idea / process to be built/constructed.

Hardware Production/Construction

Our hardware services are split into three areas:

Electrical Electro-Mechanical Mechanical
Purely electrical based work,
  • PCB Fabrication
  • Electrical Panel Assembly
  • Electrical Installation
  • Electrical Testing
 Combining mechanical and electrical components,
  • Field wiring and equipment connections
  • System Integration
  • Prototype Build
  • Functional Testing
 Purely mechanical based work,
  • Fabrication
  • System & sub-system assembly

Our in-house hardware capabilities are limited, but we do have excellent contacts that we rely on to outsource aspects which lie outside our capabilities.

Where your idea exceeds our in-house capabilities, we are happy to put you in touch with our contacts or outsource this on your behalf.

We strive to offer a turn-key service and under the majority of circumstances we will be able to develop your idea to prototype.

Our hardware services are flexible and can be co-ordinated with your needs. Examples of our hardware services include:

  • Embedded Systems / Small Scale (<1m2)
    • Where your idea can be package into a volume <1m2
    • We can produce the PCBs and/or electrical panel(s).
    • We can fabricate (in-house or outsource) the mechanical aspects.
    • We can assemble your electrical and mechanical components at our premises.
    • We can undertake functional testing at our premises.
    • We can ship your fully functioning prototype to you.

  • Package Systems / Medium Scale (1 - 10m2)
    • Where your idea is typically packaged on to a frame/skid that fits in an ISO container.
    • We can produce the PCBs and/or electrical panel(s).
    • We can accept your frame/skid at our premises.
    • We can install your electrical components at our premises.
    • We can install any remaining mechanical components at our premises.
    • We can undertake functional testing at our premises.
    • We can ship your fully functioning package to you.

  • Plant Control Systems / Large Scale(>10m2)
    • Where your idea is typically immovable.
    • We can produce the PCBs and/or electrical panel(s).
    • We can ship the electrical components to your premises.
    • We can install your electrical components at your premises.
    • We can undertake functional testing at your premises.

Get in touch to discuss which scenario works best for you.
